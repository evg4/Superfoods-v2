Super foods

What are superfoods?

Superfoods are foods, or sometimes drinks, that are highly nutritious. They are a good source of important things like protein, fibre, iron, copper, magnesium or antioxidants. They may also contain lots of vitamins and minerals and in general promote good health.

Why are they controversial?

There is no formal definition of superfoods, which means it can be misleading and people may be fooled into thinking one superfood will counteract a predominantly bad diet.

Kale

Kale is a leafy, green vegetable rich in nutrients. It's tasty in savoury dishes but also a great addition to smoothies! It's in the same family as cabbage and Brussel sprouts.

Blueberries

Many berries can be classed as superfoods. An advantage is that they can be bought fresh or frozen, so you don't have to plan ahead too much when shopping and cooking. Blueberries are great on their own as a snack; with yoghurt and honey; or even in a dessert like crumble or pie.

Almonds

Almonds and other nuts are a great alternative to a packet of crisps if you need a snack. They contain loads of fibre, as well as vitamins and antioxidants. They taste great with a square or two of dark chocolate!

Remember!

You should aim for at least 5 portions of fruit and veg per day - but the more the better!
